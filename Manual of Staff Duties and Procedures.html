Memo of Staff Duties and Procedures

Humans of Julia is a community of Julia users on discord. it also has a presence on Github. This document specify some duties and procedures which every staff member is obliged to follow. When the member joins moderation team (after satisfying the procedures in section-1), they have some duties (as defined in section-2), and procedure to perform those duties.

Section-1: Entry and Exit of Members in Staff

As of now, 9 January 2020, there is no procedure to apply to be a member in the staff. The new members are added through the process defined as follows:

A: Procedure of Entry

  1. Any member in the staff express the need of adding a new member in the moderation team.
  2. Then after the agreement by the majority (at least > 60% or members) a call for nominations is made.
  3. Every member in the team has a right to make a nomination. Members can also mention more than one names, but they should rank them while doing so. Minimum 24 hours will be given to all the members to make nomination(s).
  4. Once the nominations and discussions are concluded internally in the staff, the name which has been mentioned by the majority will be selected for voting phase. Voting will remain open for 24 hours.
  5. In voting phase, if the selected member is approved by the majority, they will be invited for joining the staff.
  6. If the member refuse to join, then the procedure from the (3) point will be repeated 😞

B: Recommendations to keep in mind while making nominations

The following recommendations are required to be followed by the members while nominating anyone for staff role:

  • Person must be from the time zone which in which existing staff members are least active.
  • Keep in mind to promote diversity in the decision.
  • No biases on the basis of race, religion, region, language, academic background, occupation, age etc. The decision should be on the basis of the individual's merit.

C: Resignation/Removal/Suspension of the member

Any member has freedom to leave the moderation staff anytime they wish. When they will leave the staff, they will be given "Retired Mod" role as a gesture of thanks for helping us.

In case of violation of community standards, mentioned in section-B or the general community standards defined by by Julia any moderator can be suspended or removed from the position. For removal or suspension, at least >65% of the staff must be in the favor of calling vote on suspension or removal of the member. A member can be suspended from the staff duties for 5 days, 1 month, 3 months, 6 months and 1 year (depending on the trouble they have caused).

Suspended or removed staff member can continue to be the part of Humans of Julia community, and will be treated as same as the other members.

Section 2: Duties of Staff

Staff on Humans of Julia comprises of Moderator, Community Manager and Admin, any new position can be introduced anytime with the agreement of existing members. Every staff member has right to take action upon violation of any of the following rules: 

​x
Simple Rules
1) No racism
2) No harassment or any kind of discrimination
3) No religion
4) No politics 
5) No spam and no advertising
6) No requesting money or digital currencies
​
Things to Consider:
​
- Act professionally and avoid bad language
- Stay on topic unless you’re in the #off-topic channel
- If you have a question about your code, please provide a snippet of the code 
- Just because someone is helping you, doesn't mean that they are writing the code for you
​
We also respect the Julia Community Standards, which is available at https://julialang.org/community/standards/

 

Following type of actions can be taken by staff:

  1. Warnings
  • Ideally, warnings should be given in private (it seems to get worse given what we've seen previously)
  • Every warning should clearly indicate what specific behavior was uncivil and how it violated our policy
  1. Muting & Banning
  • The rules is easier to follow with some pre-defined thresholds. For example, 3 warnings within a week to mute, and 3 mutes within a month to ban for 3 months.
  • If an issue deemed more severe, the rule can be overridden to accelerate the mute/ban with the agreement of a second mod.
  1. Ownership & Documentation
  • The staff that gave warning is responsible for documenting through the form.
  • Documentation should include a copy of related text and warnings/responses from that person.

Section 3: Internal Disputes

Any member facing any sort of issue can approach to the community manager, Jake Ireland, and register the complaint against the behavior of any other staff member. In this case, a committee of 4 Mods, including community manager and Admin, will seek resolution and propose action accordingly.

Section 4: Changes in the Memo

This memo can be amended anytime upon the agreement of the staff.
